Pi Tiles

Distributed matrix multiplication over a network of Raspberry Pis

In the spring of 2019, I worked with a team of other undergraduates in the Northeastern Computer Architecture Research Lab on the Pi Tiles project. We were jointly advised by a graduate student and a professor. The main goal was to gauge the effectiveness of parallel computation with the Raspberry Pis in a fairly simple application (matrix multiplication).

We utilized a block multiplication algorithm to multiply the matrices, allowing us to split the problem up into discrete chunks that could be processed in parallel. A single master node split up the matrix into these subproblems and sent them off to each slave node to be processed. After the master node received the solution to each subproblem, it would combine all of the results into the final solution.

Ultimately, in the best case, there was approximately a 10x speedup over a serial algorithm when multiplying 2 512x512 matrices

Our project won the Graduate Innovator Award at Northeastern's RISE research conference.
